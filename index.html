Projects

Kinect Tetris

During this project we used the Microsoft Kinect to alter the experience of playing Tetris. The position of a brick is controlled by the head position of the player. Bricks can be rotated with your hand and dropped with stomping motion.

Arduino meets Unity

In this project we used RFID Tags, a RFID reader and an Arduino to generate objects in Unity, a rendering engine for 3D and 2D games. Unity made it easy for us to give the spawned objects some physics properties and to interact and control them. With the blue RFID tags obstacles can be spawned and with the red RFID tag a moveable player can be created in the scene.

Pink fluffy speaking unicorn for kids

One of my personal favorites – the pink speaking unicorn project. Inside of the unicorn runs an Arduino with a radio module to send and receive data from our simple app. The necklace of the unicorn is a RFID reader and inside the laser cut fruits are RFID tags which resemble the different fruits in our app. Currently our speaking plush toy is used for therapy purposes with kids with mental retardation.

Whack-A-Mole with Play-Doh

With the UBI UBI, a self-made version of the Makey Makey by our supervisor, we merged physical input with a digital game. In this case a clone of the well-known Whack-A-Mole game was created. You can imagine the UBI UBI as a kind of keyboard which sends signals based on conductivity.

HTC Vive meets Leap Motion and Arduino

In this project we created a 3D VR multiplayer game for the HTC Vive and the Leap Motion in Unity. Additionally, we used our knowledge from working with RFID tags to include a mechanism of spawning objects in the game world using various tags. The Leap and Vive players are moved via gesture controls. Our game was honored with the best project award out of 16 other projects.

eTextiles playing music

During this project we worked with conductive textiles and build a simple prototype of a wristband or shirt sleeve which can play music based on which button is closed. I would like to point out that all of the material is still flexible and comfortably wearable.

VIP cinema seats – low fi prototype

Based on a card game which our class created in advance for “idea generation”, where you draw 4 random cards we came up with the idea of a VIP cinema seat. Our cards were: Location-Card: Cinema, Device-Card: Arduino, Input-Card: Button, Transmitting-Card: Radio Module.

Digiboard – the digital blackboard

Digiboard is our idea on how a digital blackboard could look like. We think that with our solution of a blackboard the neighborhood community gets the possibility to be connected through sharing activities and food. We think that Digiboard is also beneficial for announcements from the property management which can save them time, paper, and CO2 emission through simply posting something on Digiboard.

A Tweeting door – an IoT alarm system

In this project we used an ultra-sonic sensor to monitor if a door gets opened. If the distance from the sensor to the door changes, we send a signal to an Arduino connected to a laptop which will create a new twitter post indicating the time of opening or closing of the door.
